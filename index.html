I am a Ph.D. student in the Electrical Engineering Department, Indian Institute of Technology Bombay, advised by Prof. Subhasis Chaudhuri and Prof. Siddhartha Chaudhuri. My research goal is to model and synthesize the human multisensory (vision, speech, haptic, olfactory, and taste signals) perceptual system. To that end, my research over the past few years has focussed on developing perceptual metrics to estimate human-perceived (dis)similarity between two objects. Motivated by the specific necessity of data-efficiency in non-visual domains such as haptics and olfactory, where data gathering is considerably more difficult, I develop active metric learning algorithms, in order to minimize the training instances required for learning an effective model.

Previously, I completed my master's in Electrical Engineering at IIT Bombay, advised by Prof. Subhasis Chaudhuri. As a part of my master's thesis, I developed multimodal rendering techniques to synthesize a combined hapto-visual-auditory perceptual experience of interaction with 3D models of heritage sites. The goal was to provide access to heritage objects to visually-impaired people.

Publications:

  • A Unified Batch Selection Policy for Active Metric Learning
    ECML-PKDD, 2021
    Priyadarshini K, Siddhartha Chaudhuri, Vivek Borkar and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv]

    We propose a novel batch-mode active learning (AL) algorithm that defines a unified utility function based on conditional entropy to balance both informativeness and diversity of batches of triplets jointly. The method reduces biases in utility measures by considering multiple sampled observations from the triplet distribution learned using the Maximum Entropy Principle. Our approach requires no heuristic parameter selection and generalizes to different tasks and input modalities.

  • Batch Decorrelation for Active Metric Learning
    IJCAI-PRICAI, 2020
    Priyadarshini K, Ritesh Goru, Siddhartha Chaudhuri and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv][Code]

    We present an active learning (AL) strategy for training parametric models of distance metrics, given triplet-based similarity comparisons: object A is more similar to object B than to object C. The standard active learning approaches degrade when annotations are requested for batches of triplets due to correlation among them. In this work, we propose novel triplet distance measures for batch decorrelation and found them effective for improving the performance of existing AL strategies.

  • PerceptNet:Learning Perceptual Similarity of Haptic Textures in Presence of Unorderable Triplets
    IEEE World Haptics Conference (WHC), 2019
    Priyadarshini K, Siddhartha Chaudhuri and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv][Code]

    Modeling perceptual similarity is essential for improving the sensing capability of machines and robots. We propose a deep metric learning approach where the model is trained only on non-numerical, triplet-based relative comparisons of signals. While existing methods ignore unorderable/ambiguous signals as uninformative, our study demonstrates the value of incorporating such signals into the training process, in order to better encode the nuances and limitations of human perception. The model is trained on relatively little data and generalizes gracefully to unseen signals.

  • Haptic Rendering of Thin, Deformable Objects with Spatially Varying Stiffness
    EuroHaptics, 2016
    Priyadarshini K and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv]

    Haptic rendering a deformable object is challenging, particularly when the object has varying stiffness, such as skin and elastic sheets. We introduce a method that approximates the object by piecewise planar surfaces and leverages the classical Kirchhoff's thin plate theory to model deformation in the objects of varied geometric details.

  • Combined Hapto-Visual and Auditory Rendering of Cultural Heritage Objects
    Asian Conference on Computer Vision Workshop (ACCVw), 2014
    Praseedha K, Sreeni K, Priyadarshini K and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv]

    We develop a multi-modal rendering framework that provides the haptic, visual, and auditory perception of objects. Our algorithm effectively renders 3D objects with attributes like scaling, rotation, translation, and friction to augment the users' experience.

  • Scalable Rendering of Variable Density Point Cloud Data
    IEEE World Haptics Conference (WHC), 2013
    Priyadarshini K, Sreeni K and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv]

    The haptic rendering of thin objects usually suffers from tunneling or pop-through problem, where the proxy can traverse thin parts of the object, thereby degrading the tactile perception of structural details. We introduce a run-time kernel bandwidth estimation technique that allows the proxy radius to change adaptively based on the local object's density to avoid pop-through effects.

  • Haptic Rendering of Cultural Heritage Objects at Different Scales
    EuroHaptics, 2012
    Sreeni K, Priyadarshini K, Praseedha K and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv]

    We propose a proxy-based rendering technique to enable hapto-visual interaction with cultural heritage objects for visually impaired people. While exploring the objects, a user gets a visual and tactile perception of objects dynamically at multiple resolutions.

  • Boosted Semantic Embedding based Discriminative Feature Generation for Texture Analysis
    arXiv Preprint, 2020
    Priyadarshini K and Subhasis Chaudhuri
    [PDF][arXiv]

    We develop a discriminative feature learning framework for high-dimensional and highly correlated haptic signals. Our framework introduces a novel set of low-dimensional hand-crafted spectral features, augmented by a boosted embedding technique to improve intra-class compactness and inter-class separability.
